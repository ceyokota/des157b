Usability Testing Results

Three significant observations that emerged through testing

1. Overall, the users felt like the project was impactful and asked questions that felt relevant to their lives.

2. There were some points of confusion regarding the navigation and interaction within the project.

3. The end screen's design could be improved to better communicate the information about the future projection.

Things that can be improved:

  • include an indication that the user needs to select an option. Some users were waiting for something to happen and didn't know they were already in the quiz portion
  • the calculation at the end was not too clear on what the colored orbs meant or what the light and dark meant. Maybe add more obvious labels and indication on what they represent
  • add a restart button on the end screen
  • it wasn't clear that the end screen was scrollable
  • possibly hint in the beginning that you get to see your future projection at the end. Because the experience requires so much reading, the user would be more inclined to finish if there was an obvious reward

Can users figure out how to navigate/interact with your project?

Overall, users were able to navigate through the project and understand how to interact with it. However, there were some points of confusion, particularly when the quiz portion starts where some users were unsure if they needed to select an option or wait for something to happen. Additionally, the end screen's design led to some users not realizing that they could scroll down to see more information about their future projection.

Does the project impact the user the way you want it to? (Does it have the right message or communicate what you want it to?)

Yes, the project does impact the user in the way I intended. The quiz format effectively engages users in reflecting on their priorities and the trade-offs they make in their daily lives.

What modifications need to be made to interactions, visuals or other elements of the project to make it stronger?

As mentioned above, the end screen could be improved by adding a scroll indicator or a hint to let users know that they can scroll down to see more information about their future projection. Addtionally, improving the visual hierarchy and providing more explicit instructions could enhance the overall user experience. Lastly, the end screen can more clearly label the orbs.

Plan of how you will update your capstone project based on the feedback.

Based on the feedback, I plan to implement the following updates to my capstone project:

  • Add a clear indication at the start of the quiz to prompt users to select an option, ensuring they understand how to interact with the quiz.
  • Redesign the end screen to include a scroll indicator or a visual cue that encourages users to scroll down to view the full blurb about their future projection.
  • Enhance the visual hierarchy on the end screen to make it clearer what the colored orbs represent, possibly by adding labels or a legend.
  • Include a restart button on the end screen to allow users to easily retake the quiz and explore different outcomes.
  • Consider adding a hint at the beginning of the experience that informs users they will receive a future projection at the end, which may motivate them to complete the quiz and engage more deeply with the content.
